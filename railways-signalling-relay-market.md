<p><p>Railway signaling relay markets are categorized into DC (Direct Current) and AC (Alternating Current) segments based on the power requirements of signaling systems. DC markets primarily utilize relays powered by direct current, often found in older light rail and metro systems, offering simplicity and reliability. In contrast, AC markets employ relays designed for alternating current, which may be used in modern rail networks allowing for more complex signaling solutions. Both types ensure safe and efficient train operations, adapting to varying infrastructure needs.</p></p>
