The market includes electrochemical sensors, which detect chemical changes by measuring electrical signal variations in response to analyte interaction, enabling precise quantification. Optical sensors, on the other hand, utilize light absorption, fluorescence, or scattering techniques to identify and measure biological substances, offering high sensitivity and specificity. Both sensor types are essential for applications in healthcare, environmental monitoring, and food safety, providing rapid and accurate analysis.</p></p>
