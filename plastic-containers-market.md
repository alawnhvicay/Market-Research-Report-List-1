<p><p>The plastic containers market comprises various types, each with unique properties. Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET) is widely used for bottles due to its strength and recyclability. High Density Polyethylene (HDPE) offers durability and is ideal for containers like milk jugs. Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) is versatile but less common for food storage. Low Density Polyethylene (LDPE) is flexible and used for bags. Polypropylene (PP) is resistant to heat and chemicals, while Polystyrene (PS) is lightweight, often used for disposable items. Other resins include specialty materials catering to specific applications.</p></p>
